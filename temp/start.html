What You Need to Start

  1. Woodenware

    2. Woodenware include things such as your hive, frames, extra supers, and hive stand. These things will make up the house of the bees, and they will live inside of them.

  2. Protective gear

    3. Protective gear like a face net and glives are crucial for a beginner. When you arent comfortable with the bees and they arent comfortable with you, doing tasks while worrying about getting stung will be a very hard thing to do.

  3. Tools
    4. "Hive Boxes and Bee Keeping Equipment" by Terrie Schweitzer is licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 2.0

    Tools make the whole process of opening and caring for your hive a hwole lot easier. You could make due with some makeshift tools like a screwdriver or a crowbar, but getting tools that are designed to be for beekeepers will make the whole proces smuch easier.

  4. Bees

    5. And of course, wo will need your bees. The most common breed is the italian honey bee, or Apis mellifera. There are 8 different types of honey bee though, not counting hybrids, and each will be a different experience, and will produce a different yield. You can buy bees either online or at a local retailer.
